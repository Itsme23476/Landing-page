Filect vs Dropbox Dash

Updated May 2026

Summary

Filect is an AI-powered desktop file search and organization app for local files on Windows and Mac. Dropbox Dash is an AI-powered universal search tool that connects to cloud services like Google Drive, Slack, Notion, and Gmail. They solve different problems: Filect organizes your hard drive, Dash searches your cloud apps.

What Filect Does

Filect is an AI desktop file organizer and search tool. It reads the content of every document on your local hard drive (PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets, code files, images with text) and creates a semantic index. You search by describing what you're looking for in plain language, and Filect returns matching files regardless of filename or folder location.

Key strengths:

  • Searches local files by meaning, not just filename
  • Automatically categorizes files by content topic
  • Handles scanned documents via OCR
  • "The invoice from the plumber" finds scan_004.pdf

What Dropbox Dash Does

Dropbox Dash is a universal search bar for cloud applications. It connects to services like Google Drive, Slack, Notion, Salesforce, Figma, and others, and lets you search across all of them from one interface. It uses AI to understand queries and surface relevant results from any connected app.

Key strengths:

  • Searches across 20+ cloud services simultaneously
  • AI-powered result ranking
  • Smart summaries of search results
  • Strong integration ecosystem

Key Differences

Local vs Cloud

This is the fundamental difference. Filect works with files on your hard drive. Dropbox Dash works with files in cloud apps. If your documents are PDFs and Word files saved to your computer, Filect is the right tool. If your documents are in Google Drive, Notion, and Slack, Dash is the right tool.

Organization vs Search

Filect does both: it organizes files by content and provides search. Dropbox Dash is primarily a search tool. It finds things across cloud apps but doesn't organize or categorize them.

File Content Understanding

Both tools use AI to understand content. The difference is what content they access. Filect reads local files directly (including scanned PDFs via OCR). Dash reads whatever the connected cloud apps expose through their APIs.

Verdict

Choose Filect if: Your files live on your computer. You have thousands of local PDFs, documents, and downloads that need organization and search. You want AI to read inside your documents and categorize them automatically.

Choose Dropbox Dash if: Your work lives in cloud apps. You need to search across Google Drive, Slack, Notion, and other SaaS tools from one place.

Use both if: You have important files both locally and in the cloud. They don't overlap and solve complementary problems.
