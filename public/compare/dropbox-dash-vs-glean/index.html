Dropbox Dash vs Glean

Updated July 2026

Summary

Dropbox Dash and Glean both use AI to search across your work apps, but they target different buyers. Dash is the lighter option: self-serve setup, per-user pricing, built for teams that live in common tools like Google Workspace and Slack. Glean is the enterprise heavyweight: deeper integrations, permissions-aware AI answers, and custom pricing that assumes an IT department. Neither one searches the files on your own computer.

What Dropbox Dash Does

Dash is Dropbox's answer to scattered work content. Connect your team's tools (Google Workspace, Slack, Notion, and others) and Dash gives you one AI-powered search bar across all of them, plus AI answers drawn from your connected content.

Its strength is accessibility. A team lead can set it up without involving IT, pricing is published and per-user, and it works well for the common stack most small teams already use.

Its ceiling: integration depth and enterprise controls are lighter than Glean's, which matters once a company has dozens of systems and complex permissions.

What Glean Does

Glean is enterprise search done seriously. It connects to a very large catalog of workplace systems, indexes everything with permissions intact (you only see results you're allowed to see), and layers an AI assistant on top that can answer questions from your company's collective knowledge.

Its strength is depth and trust at scale. Large companies choose Glean because it handles messy, permission-heavy environments correctly.

Its ceiling: it's an enterprise sale. Custom quotes, IT-led deployment, and a price point that makes sense for hundreds of seats, not five.

Which One Should You Pick?

Choose Dropbox Dash if:

  • You're a small or mid-size team on common SaaS tools
  • You want self-serve setup and published pricing
  • You already use Dropbox

Choose Glean if:

  • You're an enterprise with many systems and strict permissions
  • You want the deepest integration catalog and mature AI answers
  • You have IT resources for deployment and an enterprise budget
