Summary

Hazel (by Noodlesoft) is the classic Mac automation tool: it watches folders and applies rules you write to move, rename, and sort files. Filect uses AI to understand what files contain, organizes them without any rules, and adds natural language search across your whole drive. Hazel rewards people who like building systems. Filect is for people who just want the outcome.

What Hazel Does

Hazel has been automating Mac folders for over 15 years. You point it at a folder and define rules: if the filename contains "invoice," move it to Finance and rename it by date. If a download is older than 30 days, archive it. Rules run silently in the background, forever.

Its power users build elaborate systems: rules that read text inside PDFs, chain conditions together, and trigger AppleScript. Once dialed in, Hazel is famously reliable, and the one-time license means you pay once.

The cost is the setup. Every pattern you want handled is a rule you write, test, and maintain. Files that don't match any rule just sit there, and those unpredictable files are usually why folders get messy in the first place.

What Filect Does

Filect reads the actual content of your files with AI, so it knows an invoice is an invoice even when the filename is scan_0233.pdf. It categorizes and organizes files automatically with no rules to write, and applies AI tags.

It also does the thing rules never could: search. Describe a file the way you remember it ("the contract we signed with the landlord in March") and Filect finds it in seconds, even inside screenshots and scans thanks to OCR. It works on both Windows and Mac.

The tradeoff: it's a subscription rather than a one-time purchase, and it doesn't offer Hazel-style custom scripted actions like automated rename chains or AppleScript triggers.

Key Difference

Hazel is about executing your system. You decide the logic; Hazel runs it with total precision. If your files arrive in predictable patterns, that's a great deal.

Filect is about skipping the system entirely. The AI decides what each file is by reading it, which means it handles the files no rule anticipated, and it makes everything findable afterward.

Honest test: if you enjoy writing and refining rules, Hazel will make you happy on a Mac. If you want clean, findable files and zero upkeep, or you're on Windows where Hazel doesn't exist, Filect is the better fit.
