What Fabric Does

Fabric is an AI-powered "second brain" designed for collecting and organizing information from the internet. You save bookmarks, screenshots, notes, PDFs, and links into Fabric, and its AI automatically organizes everything, finds connections between items, and makes it all searchable.

It's built for creative professionals, researchers, and writers who collect lots of information and need to find it later. Think of it as a smarter version of browser bookmarks combined with a note-taking app.

Key strength: excellent for research workflows. You clip things from the web, Fabric organizes them, and you can search everything later.

What Filect Does

Filect is an AI-powered desktop file search and organization tool. It reads the actual files already sitting on your hard drive (PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets, images, code files) and creates a semantic index. You search by describing what you're looking for, and Filect finds matching files regardless of their filename or folder location.

It also auto-categorizes files by content topic and applies AI-generated tags. You don't save anything into Filect. It reads your existing files where they are.

Key strength: making thousands of existing files instantly searchable without changing your workflow.

The Core Difference

Fabric organizes things you actively save into it. You clip a webpage, screenshot a quote, or drop a PDF into Fabric. If you don't save it, Fabric doesn't know about it.

Filect organizes things that are already on your computer. It automatically reads every file on your drive. You don't need to save anything. Every PDF, document, and image is already indexed and searchable.

This is a fundamentally different approach. Fabric requires manual input. Filect works automatically on what you already have.

Who Should Use Which

Choose Fabric if:

  • You need to collect and organize web research
  • You want an AI-powered bookmark and clipping system
  • You're a content creator who saves lots of references
  • Your workflow is about collecting new information

Choose Filect if:

  • You have thousands of files on your computer you can't find
  • Your Downloads folder is full of PDFs with meaningless names
  • You need to search inside documents by describing their content
  • Your workflow is about finding existing files, not collecting new ones

Use both if:

You collect research online (Fabric) and also have messy local files (Filect). They solve completely different problems and don't overlap at all.
